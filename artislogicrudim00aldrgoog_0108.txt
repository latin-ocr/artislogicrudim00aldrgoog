The error has been caused by the Aristotelian expression,
ἀπαγωγὴ εἰς πὸ dodum in which, however, ἀπαγωγὴ does not
mean reduction. The deductio od impossibile, as it is usually
rendered, (obductio would perhaps be betten) is one species
of the συλλογισμὸς ἐξ ὑποθέσεως, (see Appendix, note G‚) the
object of which is, to prove the truth of a given problem, by
inferring a falsehood from the assumption of its contradictory.
This may beemployed in the reduction of syllogisms but it
is also used for other purposes, as by Geometers. (Euchd. i. 7.)
The correct expression is therefore Reductio per deductionem
od impossibile, or elliptioally, Reductio per impossibile. The
ἀπαγωγὴ of An. Pr. ii. 25. will be explained hereafter.

Any mood may be reduced by the deductio od impossibile,
though in practice it is usually confined to Baroko and
Bokardo.
